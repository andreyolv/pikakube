# Data Catalog & Data Governance with OpenMetadata

## Problem:
- Lack of Data Discoverability: As data platforms grow, teams struggle to find trusted datasets, understand ownership, and reuse existing data assets.
- Poor Metadata Visibility: Technical, operational, and business metadata are often scattered across tools, making it difficult to understand context, freshness, and reliability.
- Data Trust Issues: Without clear ownership, documentation, and quality signals, analysts and stakeholders lose confidence in data products.
- Governance at Scale: Enforcing data governance, classifications, and access policies becomes increasingly complex across multiple teams and pipelines.

## Solution:
- Centralized Data Catalog with OpenMetadata: Implemented OpenMetadata as a unified data catalog to centralize datasets, dashboards, pipelines, and ML models in a single platform.
- Automated Metadata Ingestion: Integrated OpenMetadata with data sources, query engines, orchestration tools, and BI platforms to automatically ingest technical and operational metadata.
- Ownership & Documentation: Enabled dataset ownership, rich descriptions, tags, and domains to improve accountability and collaboration across teams.
- Data Lineage Integration: Leveraged OpenMetadata’s lineage capabilities to provide upstream and downstream visibility, supporting impact analysis and faster troubleshooting.
- Data Quality & Observability Signals: Integrated data quality checks and freshness indicators to increase trust and reliability in data assets.
- overnance & Classification: Applied data classifications, sensitivity labels, and policies to support governance, compliance, and controlled data access.
- Scalable Metadata Platform: Designed the catalog to scale across domains and teams, supporting a data mesh–oriented operating model.
