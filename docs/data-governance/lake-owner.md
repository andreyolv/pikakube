# Data Lake Ownership and Accountability Framework

## Problem:
- Lack of Clear Ownership: Data lakes often grow organically without defined owners, making it unclear who is responsible for data correctness, availability, and evolution.
- Slow Incident Resolution: When data issues occur, the absence of accountable owners leads to long investigation cycles and finger-pointing across teams.
- Low Data Trust: Consumers hesitate to rely on datasets when there is no visible owner guaranteeing data quality, freshness, or business meaning.
- Governance Gaps: Without ownership, enforcing access policies, retention rules, and compliance requirements becomes inconsistent and error-prone.
- Scalability Challenges: As the number of datasets and domains increases, centralized ownership models fail to scale.

## Solution:
- Dataset Ownership Model: Defined a clear ownership model for the data lake, assigning owners at dataset, domain, or data product level.
- Domain-Oriented Ownership: Aligned ownership with business domains or teams, enabling decentralized responsibility while maintaining global governance standards.
- Owner Metadata Exposure: Surfaced ownership information (team, contact, escalation path) directly in data catalogs and documentation.
- Operational Accountability: Made owners responsible for data quality, schema evolution, freshness SLAs, and incident response.
- Governance Integration: Linked ownership to access control, data classification, retention policies, and compliance enforcement.
- Improved Incident Management: Enabled faster root cause analysis and resolution by clearly identifying accountable owners for each dataset.
- Scalable Data Platform: Established a foundation that scales with data growth, supporting data mesh and data product-oriented architectures.
